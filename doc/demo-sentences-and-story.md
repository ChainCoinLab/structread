# Demo Sentences And Story

This document defines the representative sentence set and the recommended product storytelling flow for `StructRead`.

## Core Story

StructRead should not be introduced as a grammar-labeling tool only.

The clearest story is:

1. show one difficult original sentence
2. extract the main sentence first
3. unpack long modifiers and clauses into smaller meaning units
4. rewrite the sentence as several short readable ideas

The product promise is:

`StructRead helps readers see the main sentence first, then unpack long modifiers and clauses into smaller meaning units.`

Alternative short version:

`It does not just label grammar. It turns one difficult sentence into several readable short ideas.`

## Recommended Demo Set

Use these three sentences as the primary public demo set.

They cover:

- relative clause structures
- concessive or adverbial clause structures
- long object and explanation structures

## Demo Sentence 1

### Original sentence

`The experienced researchers at the university, who had spent years studying language acquisition, developed a practical method that helps non-native readers understand complex English sentences more quickly.`

### Why this sentence works

- clear main sentence
- long relative clause after the subject
- long `that` clause after the object
- ideal for showing first-level and second-level decomposition

### Main sentence

`Researchers developed a practical method.`

### Secondary structure

1. `who had spent years studying language acquisition`
2. `that helps non-native readers understand complex English sentences more quickly`

### Short-sentence rewrites

1. `The researchers had spent years studying language acquisition.`
2. `The method helps non-native readers understand complex English sentences more quickly.`

### Best use

- hero demo
- first screenshot
- main marketing page example

## Demo Sentence 2

### Original sentence

`Although the committee had already approved the proposal, several members who had previously remained silent began raising concerns about its long-term cost.`

### Why this sentence works

- good contrast between the main clause and the opening concessive clause
- contains a relative clause inside the subject area
- contains a long support phrase after the verb
- ideal for showing how non-core parts can be weakened visually first, then unpacked

### Main sentence

`Several members began raising concerns.`

### Secondary structure

1. `Although the committee had already approved the proposal`
2. `who had previously remained silent`
3. `about its long-term cost`

### Short-sentence rewrites

1. `The committee had already approved the proposal.`
2. `Those members had previously remained silent.`
3. `Their concerns were about its long-term cost.`

### Best use

- learning mode screenshot
- structure hierarchy demo
- explanation panel example

## Demo Sentence 3

### Original sentence

`Many students find the writing style used in academic articles difficult because the main idea is often hidden inside long sentences with several layers of modifiers.`

### Why this sentence works

- directly matches the user's real learning pain point
- simple main sentence with a meaningful cause clause
- good for positioning the product as a reading tool, not only a syntax tool

### Main sentence

`Many students find the writing style difficult.`

### Secondary structure

1. `used in academic articles`
2. `because the main idea is often hidden inside long sentences with several layers of modifiers`

### Short-sentence rewrites

1. `This writing style is used in academic articles.`
2. `The main idea is often hidden inside long sentences.`
3. `Those sentences contain several layers of modifiers.`

### Best use

- problem statement
- value proposition section
- educational landing page copy

## Recommended Presentation Format

For each sentence, present the information in this order:

### 1. Original sentence

Show the full sentence in its natural form.

### 2. Main sentence

Show only:

- subject
- verb
- object

This must be the strongest visual layer.

### 3. Secondary structure

Show the long modifiers and clauses as lower-emphasis units.

These should appear as:

- support detail
- secondary clause
- descriptive phrase

### 4. Short-sentence rewrites

Convert the long sentence into 2 to 4 short readable ideas.

This is the most important comprehension payoff.

### Option B

### Option C

`Read English by structure, not guesswork.`

## Usage Guidance

Use the three demo sentences consistently across:

- Chrome Web Store screenshots
- promo graphics
- homepage sections
- reviewer demo notes
- product walkthroughs

Avoid using random sentences across different materials. Repetition helps users understand the product faster and makes the visual system feel intentional.
